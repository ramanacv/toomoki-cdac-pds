India’s Public Distribution System (PDS) has undergone a series of rapid digital transformations. The introduction of **SMART-PDS** (focused on tech-driven reforms, data ministration, and smart weighing scales) and its recent integration into the massive umbrella scheme **SARTHAK-PDS** (approved by the Union Cabinet in May 2026 with an outlay of ₹25,530 crore to introduce AI, blockchain, and machine learning) represent major strides.

However, external research and ground realities highlight that technology has not completely solved structural gaps. Recent data—including a widely discussed **ICRIER report** indicating a roughly 28% leakage rate of subsidized grains, alongside an analysis by the **Tata-Cornell Institute (TCI)** utilizing the latest Household Consumption and Expenditure Survey (HCES)—shows that distinct "slippages" persist.

The primary forms of leakage, fraud, and loss that SMART-PDS and the early rollouts of SARTHAK-PDS have struggled to fix include:

---

## 1. Biometric & Connectivity Failures (Exclusion Errors)

While identity management was digitized to stop fraud, it introduced severe technological exclusion errors that digital tracking cannot natively fix.

* **The "Manual Labor" and Ageing Barrier:** Elderly beneficiaries and manual laborers frequently suffer from worn-out fingerprints. When Aadhaar-based biometric authentication (AePBA) fails at electronic Point of Sale (e-PoS) devices, genuine beneficiaries are turned away.
* **The Connectivity Shadow:** In remote, tribal, or hilly regions (e.g., parts of Jharkhand, Odisha, and the Northeast), irregular cellular data and frequent power outages stall e-PoS devices. Dealerships often bypass the system or force users to make multiple trips, causing a logistical loss to the poor.

## 2. In-Transit Diversion & The "Last Mile" Black Hole

Smart tracking systems monitor grain movement from Food Corporation of India (FCI) godowns to state hubs, but the transition from regional warehouses to the individual Fair Price Shop (FPS) remains highly vulnerable.

* **Route Deviations:** While state trucks might be fitted with GPS, tracking loops frequently fail right before the final destination. High-quality subsidized wheat and rice are diverted to the open commercial market, replaced at the shop level with substandard, heavily broken, or insect-infested grain.
* **The "Weight "Slippage:** Even with electronic weighing scales linked directly to e-PoS machines, dealers find physical workarounds. A common reporting trick is "forced under-weighing"—where a beneficiary is given 4 kg instead of their allotted 5 kg, but their biometric thumbprint authorizes the transaction in full. The leftover grain is pocketed and sold off the record.

## 3. Data Overlaps and "Ghost" Card Resurgence

A primary goal of SMART-PDS was using Aadhaar to scrub fake or duplicate cards. However, dynamic population changes have outpaced static databases.

* **The Delayed Census Trap:** PDS allocations are technically tethered to the outdated 2011 Census metrics. Because of this, over 90 million eligible individuals are excluded from formal benefits.
* **Dynamic Mutation Gaps:** When a cardholder passes away or a woman moves after marriage, there is a substantial administrative lag before databases sync. Rogue dealers exploit this window, continuing to log biometric bypasses (using emergency manual overrides) to claim rations for these "ghost" or deceased individuals.

## 4. The Migrant Portability Friction

Under the One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC) framework integrated into these smart systems, migrant workers should be able to buy split rations anywhere in India. In practice, serious friction remains.

* **Local Protectionism and Stock-Outs:** FPS dealers operate on tight monthly allocations based on historical local data. When a surge of migrant workers attempts to claim rations in an industrial cluster or metro city, the shop quickly experiences a stock-out.
* Because dealers prioritize their permanent local clientele to maintain community ties, migrant populations face high rejection rates.

## 5. High Storage Infrastructure & Moisture Losses

Technology at the point of distribution cannot fix systemic decay upstream.

* **Warehousing Inefficiencies:** Millions of tonnes of grain are lost annually to damp conditions, rodents, and open-cap storage (grains stored under tarpaulins outside) before they ever reach an e-PoS device.
* **The Weight Magic of Moisture:** Grains naturally lose weight as they dry out during prolonged transport and storage. This creates a legal gray area: dealers complain that the physical bag weight they receive is less than the digital manifest sent via the PDS app, forcing them to distribute less to individuals to break even.
