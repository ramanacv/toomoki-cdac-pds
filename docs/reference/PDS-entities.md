Based on the sources, the supply chain for food distribution is a multi-layered system involving governmental, regulatory, technical, and retail entities. The following distinct entities form the chain from start to finish:

### 1. Central Policy and Procurement Entities
*   **Central Government / Department of Food and Public Distribution (DFPD):** Provides the national policy framework, manages the National Food Security Act (NFSA) portal, and oversees the overall food economy.
*   **Food Corporation of India (FCI):** The central operational arm responsible for the **procurement, storage, and bulk transportation** of foodgrains to the states.

### 2. State Administration and Resource Management
*   **State Government (e.g., Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department, Maharashtra):** Manages operational responsibilities including state-level allocation, identification of eligible families, and issuing **Ration Cards**.
*   **Divisional and District Offices (DSO/FDO):** Local administrative units (such as Ahmadnagar, Nashik, or Pune) that manage regional quotas and monitor supply chain activities.

### 3. Logistics and Storage Nodes
*   **Government Godowns and Depots:** Strategic storage facilities where bulk stock is received and held before being moved into the local distribution pipeline.
*   **Issue Points:** Specific locations from which foodgrains are dispatched to the next level in the chain, such as retail shops or welfare centers.

### 4. Retail and Frontline Distribution Endpoints
*   **Fair Price Shops (FPS):** Also known as Raastabhav Dukane (रास्तभाव दुकाने), these are the primary retail outlets where beneficiaries purchase subsidized foodgrains.
*   **Shiv Bhojan Eateries:** Specialized government-subsidized centers that provide cooked meals to the public.
*   **Welfare Institutions:** Non-individual recipients such as **hostels and ashram schools** that receive foodgrains at Below Poverty Line (BPL) rates for their inmates.

### 5. Regulatory and Oversight Entities
*   **Legal Metrology Department (Vajne-Mape):** Responsible for ensuring fairness in the system by verifying and stamping **weights and measures** used at distribution points and licensing manufacturers and repairers of weighing equipment.
*   **Vigilance Committees:** Local oversight bodies established to monitor the transparency and functioning of the Public Distribution System (PDS).

### 6. Technical and Infrastructure Providers
*   **National Informatics Centre (NIC):** The technical partner that designs, develops, and hosts the digital infrastructure, including the **AePDS**, **IAeSCM**, and **NFSA** portals.
*   **UIDAI:** Provides the **Aadhaar** biometric authentication services required for beneficiaries to collect their rations through electronic Point of Sale (e-PoS) devices.

### 7. The Consumer (Beneficiaries)
*   **Ration Card Holders:** Categorized into groups such as **Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY)** (poorest of the poor), **Priority Households (PHH)**, and **Non-Priority Households (NPH)** who are the final recipients of the commodities.

The physical movement of commodities between these entities is tracked through specific supply chain milestones: **Allocation** (setting quotas), **Release Orders (RO)** (authorizing movement), **Dispatch**, **Receipt** at nodes, and finally **Issued Quantity** to the consumer.

In the food distribution system, **depots and godowns** serve as critical intermediate storage nodes between bulk procurement and retail distribution. They fall under the jurisdiction of two primary entities depending on their function in the chain:

### 1. Food Corporation of India (FCI) - Central Government
The **Central Government**, operating through the **Food Corporation of India (FCI)**, is responsible for the primary storage of foodgrains after procurement.
*   **FCI Buffer Godowns:** These are large-scale storage facilities used for maintaining national food security reserves and managing bulk transportation to various states.

### 2. Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department - State Government
Once foodgrains are allocated to a state like Maharashtra, the operational responsibility for storage shifts to the **State Government**. 
*   **Government Godowns (Shaaskiya Godame):** These are managed by the state's Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection Department. 
*   **Scale of Operations:** As of June 2026, there are **492 depots** active within the Maharashtra supply chain system.

### Role of Depots in the Supply Chain
Depots are the primary units for **Stage-II Stock Movement**, where the following activities are tracked through the **Integrated Aadhaar enabled Supply Chain Management (IAeSCM)** system:
*   **RO Status:** Monitoring "Release Orders" (RO) which authorize the movement of stock.
*   **Dispatch and Receipt:** Managing the physical "Dispatch" of goods to Fair Price Shops or Welfare Institutions and recording the "Receipt" of bulk grains from the FCI.
*   **Inventory Management:** Maintaining real-time data on **Depot Stock** to ensure there are no shortages at the regional level.
*   **Issue Points:** Depots often function as the official "Issue Points" where local retailers or welfare agencies collect their allotted commodities.
